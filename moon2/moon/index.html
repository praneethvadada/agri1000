About precison farming

Precision farming is a method of farming that uses advanced technologies to collect and analyze data to improve crop and resource management. It can help farmers make more efficient and precise decisions at every stage of production.

Technologies used

Global position system (Gps)

GPS technology in precision agriculture plays a critical role in enabling accurate field mapping, crop monitoring, and precise application of inputs. By using GPS-equipped machinery, farmers can pinpoint exact locations within their fields, ensuring that fertilizers, pesticides, and irrigation are applied with high accuracy. This technology useful farmers for monitoring the field.

          

Satellite Imagery

Satellite imagery in precision agriculture enables farmers to monitor and manage crop health, soil moisture, and field conditions with high accuracy. By using remote sensing technologies like NDVI, farmers can detect early signs of stress, optimize irrigation, and predict crop yields. Variable Rate Technology (VRT)

Variable Rate Technology (VRT) in precision agriculture allows farmers to apply inputs such as fertilizers, pesticides, and water at varying rates across different areas of a field based on specific needs.
